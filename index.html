Campaign Overview

What Unravel Does

Students rely on fast fashion because it feels cheap, trendy, and easy. Unravel challenges the belief that sustainability is a luxury, and reframes it as creativity, culture, and community practice on campus.

The Basics

What
A sustainability campaign rethinking fast fashion and the stories behind what we wear.
Why
Because affordable ≠ responsible. Sustainability should not be framed as a privilege, but as an everyday practice open to all students.
Who
NU-Q and Education City students, 18–25, fashion-curious, social-media savvy, and budget-conscious.
Where
NU-Q campus, Education City spaces, Instagram & TikTok, and pop-up activations.
When
A 4-week rollout during the academic semester.
How
Cinematic storytelling, hands-on workshops, creative re-styling challenges, and activations that turn awareness into action.
Story-driven Student-led Qatar-specific Environment & ethics

Key Insight & Target Shift

Key insight: Sustainability in fashion isn’t about spending more – it’s about understanding the value behind what we wear.

When students see clothing as both expression and responsibility, style becomes a statement of awareness, not excess. Unravel moves students from “I can’t afford to care” to “I can show who I am by how I care.”

The campaign reframes sustainable fashion as:

  • Creative — upcycling, re-styling, gym wear re-design.
  • Cultural — connecting fashion with identity and local designers.
  • Community-based — swaps, workshops, and peer-to-peer inspiration.

SWOT Snapshot

Strength

  • Fashion-forward student culture.
  • Strong digital presence & content creation skills.
  • Students value individuality and self-expression.

Weakness

  • Sustainable fashion perceived as expensive or elitist.
  • Fast fashion is normalized and socially rewarded.
  • Limited access to local thrift or slow-fashion options.

Opportunity

  • Rising interest in workshops, DIY, and customization.
  • Partnerships with local sustainable brands.
  • Collaboration with VCUarts & M7 for craft-based activations.

Threat

  • “Green fatigue” — repetitive sustainability messaging.
  • Risk of campaign being seen as moralistic or privileged.
  • Students’ budget limitations and time pressure.

Experience the Campaign

Core Activations

Each activation translates the problem into a concrete experience — from redesigning your own gym top, to walking a sustainable runway, to confronting the true cost of a 20 QAR outfit.

Activation 1

ReDesign Lab — Gym Wear Edition

Students bring an old gym top, leggings, hoodie, or sportswear they no longer use and redesign it from scratch. With guidance from designer Dana Alzian, they learn how sustainable fashion works in real life — not through a lecture, but by transforming their own clothes.

The session ends with a fun Pilates class to “test” the redesigned outfits and a branded photo corner for Instagram-ready shots.

↳ Bring a piece · Leave with a new story
Activation 2

Re•Runway — Sustainable Fashion Show

A runway that celebrates NU-Q and EC students who have ever created a sustainable piece. If you have upcycled, reworked, or crafted something from reused materials — this is your stage.

Featuring: upcycled clothing, repurposed garments, reworked gym wear, accessories from recycled textiles, and senior project pieces with a sustainability focus.

↳ “If you ever created something sustainable, this is your stage.”
Activation 3

“The 20 QAR Outfit — Would You Still Buy It?”

A hidden-camera style booth promises a full outfit for just 20 QAR. Before students can “buy” it, they watch a short video revealing the real cost: garment worker conditions, underpayment, and environmental pollution.

At the end, the screen asks: “Knowing this, would you still buy it?” Students choose to buy the outfit or donate to a sustainable cause instead.

↳ Confront the real price of “cheap”
Beyond the three main activations, Unravel also lives on campus screens, Instagram challenges (like the #RewearChallenge: one item, three outfits, tag three friends), and ongoing workshops such as “Thread of Identity” and “Behind the Seams”.
